Two Trades in a Geopolitical Tape: INTC's $100 Floor vs. SPCX's IPO Tightrope

The highest-conviction setup today is INTC's approach to the $100 psychological floor. The stock has shed -6.12% to $103.12 in a single session — a 'sell-the-news' reversal following the WSJ White House-involvement story and after shares tripled year-to-date. The fundamental thesis (Apple chip integration confirmed, Intel 18A ramp ahead of schedule, Q1 revenue +7% YoY and a 650bps gross margin beat) remains intact, but the market is aggressively pricing in risk nine days before the July 23 Q2 print. The key judgment: $100 is not arbitrary — it represents a 50% round-trip from the prior base and will attract systematic buy programs if held on a closing basis. Tactically, risk/reward favors a defined-risk long entry at $100–$102 targeting a post-earnings recovery toward $115 (TD Cowen's new target), with a hard stop on a close below $98. The binary risk is clear: a Q2 miss on gross margin (guided at 39% midpoint vs. Q1's 41% beat) would invalidate the setup.

The second high-conviction setup is SPCX at $139.14 — just $4.14 above its $135 IPO price. This is a structural, not fundamental, risk. The performance-trigger for the bonus 10% lock-up tranche ($175.50 for 5 of 10 consecutive days) is completely out of reach at current prices, meaning the base 20% insider unlock after Q2 earnings remains the primary supply event. With roughly 95% of shares still locked and the float at ~5%, even the base 20% release represents a proportionally massive supply injection against thin liquidity. The correct trade posture is avoidance below $145; below $135, the IPO floor becomes the primary battleground and could trigger a technical collapse toward $120–$125 as stop-losses cascade through a thin float.

The key risk to watch across the entire portfolio today is the 10-year yield. Geopolitical Hormuz tension has already pushed yields higher by ~10bps and reignited inflation expectations. If that move accelerates into the close — driven by continued oil strength — the MSFT (+1.53%) and AAPL (+0.63%) outperformers will lose their defensive cushion, V's payment-network bid will fade, and VZ's yield spread will narrow. The one name that benefits unambiguously from this scenario is WMT at $114.78: it is the only ticker in this watchlist with both defensive qualities and a confirmed Morningstar-cited structural cost advantage that is independent of rate levels.

For speculative names: ONDS at $6.96 is untradeable today in either direction — record short interest, 107M share volume, and Form 424B7 ATM shelf filings create a squeeze/dilution binary with no clean resolution. RBLX at $55.03 on only 6.3M shares is the quietest name in the book, which is itself informative: the absence of conviction suggests it is not yet the market's focus, and traders should wait for a confirmed volume expansion above 10M before taking a position.
