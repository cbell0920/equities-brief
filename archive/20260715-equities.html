Market Regime
CPI Relief Rally
Tuesday's June CPI print — headline down 0.4% MoM vs. a -0.2% consensus, annual rate 3.5% vs. 3.8% expected, with core flat at 0.0% MoM and 2.6% YoY — delivered the broadest inflation beat since April 2020 and instantly repriced Fed-hike odds lower, with CME FedWatch showing an ~86% probability of a hold at the late-July FOMC meeting. That macro relief is carrying directly into Wednesday's session: QQQ +1.12% and NVDA +4.06% lead the charge, semiconductors are broadly bid, and BAC's blowout Q2 beat — EPS $1.21 vs. $1.13 est., revenue $31.56B vs. $30.73B est. — adds a financial-sector tailwind that keeps the risk-on tone intact despite a Extreme Fear reading of 25/100 on the Fear & Greed index.

B1 Confluences Holding the Line — GOOG, HOOD, V, and NVDA Are the Structure Worth Watching

The B1 cohort — tickers where all three timeframes (daily, weekly, monthly) show bullish EMA alignment — is the most structurally sound subset of today's watchlist: SPY (751.83), QQQ (719.71), GOOG (357.33), V (356.02), HOOD (113.45), and NVDA (211.80) all carry confirmed B1 status per the IOF confluence data. In a session where the Fear & Greed index sits at 25 (Extreme Fear), the fact that these six names are holding — and in most cases advancing — speaks to the durability of their underlying EMA structure even against a deeply pessimistic sentiment backdrop. Across all six B1 names, the macro lift from the CPI print is not creating the alignment; it is simply allowing structures already in place to express themselves.

GOOG at 357.33 (+1.90%) is the standout performer among B1 names today, running the second-largest gain on the watchlist outside of NVDA and INTC. With Q2 earnings due July 22 and the prior Q1 print showing $109.9B in revenue (+22% YoY) and a consensus EPS miss that was actually a massive beat in absolute terms ($5.11 vs. $2.62), the B1 structure heading into earnings is a textbook pre-catalyst setup. HOOD at 113.45 (+3.27%) is the second most compelling B1 setup: the stock is up approximately 30% in the past month per recent coverage, volume at 17.9M is elevated, and Q2 earnings are confirmed for July 29 with consensus revenue of $1.25B — the B1 alignment here is being backed by real momentum, not just macro drift. V at 356.02 (-0.48%) is the only B1 name pulling back today, but in a risk-on tape where financials are broadly strong post-BAC earnings, that minor intraday softness does not disturb the weekly and monthly EMA alignment.

NVDA at 211.80 (+4.06%) occupies a special category: its MWD reads B1 (daily, weekly, and monthly all bullish), but the short-term EMA is classified as mixed — a condition that often occurs when the daily price action is oscillating around the EMA20 while the longer-term structure remains firmly oriented to the upside. With 124M shares trading hands today (the heaviest single-name volume on the entire watchlist), institutional participation is unambiguous. The call-heavy OI skew on near-dated expirations, combined with NVDA's B1 MWD reading, provides a structurally and volume-confirmed picture ahead of the August 26 earnings date. The AI chip demand narrative — corroborated by Intel's own Data Center & AI segment growing 22% in Q1 — is clearly not abating.

Two tickers outside the B1 group merit specific mention because of today's distinct catalysts. INTC at 107.76 (+4.50%) is running on 91M shares with KeyBanc raising its price target to $155 yesterday and Q2 earnings scheduled for July 23 — the EMA structure is mixed across all timeframes, and the €5B Ireland campus expansion and 18A process node readiness are the fundamental drivers being repriced into the stock. NFLX at 73.53 (-0.41%) is the sharpest structural outlier: bearish EMA alignment, mixed weekly and monthly readings, and declining into a Q2 earnings print tomorrow (July 16, after market, consensus EPS $0.79, revenue $12.57B) — the only major name on the watchlist with a confirmed bearish EMA configuration heading into a same-week earnings event.
