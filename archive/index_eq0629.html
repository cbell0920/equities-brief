Senior Analyst Note
TPTraders Senior Equities Analyst — June 26, 2026

The AI trade is alive — and it just got two data points in one week to prove it. Micron's blowout earnings Wednesday confirmed AI memory chip demand is surging. TSMC's raised revenue forecast Thursday confirmed the same from the manufacturing side. These aren't coincidences — they're the same underlying story: hyperscalers are spending $700 billion+ on AI infrastructure in 2026 and the semiconductor supply chain is feeling every dollar of it. NVDA, MU, SNDK, and QCOM are the clearest expressions of this theme.

The rates headwind is real but manageable. PCE at 4.1% keeps the Fed hawkish and the 10-year yield elevated at 4.408%. That compresses multiples. But the earnings growth in AI names is outpacing the multiple compression. The S&P 500 tech sector was up 27% over the past three months even with rising rates — earnings power is overwhelming the discount rate headwind for now.

Today's session is about staying disciplined, not adding risk. Friday is low volume, end-of-week positioning. The week's big events (Micron earnings, PCE) are behind us. UMich at 10 AM is the only live catalyst. If the 5-year inflation expectations component in UMich comes in high (above 3.5%), it could briefly spike VIX and pressure semis. That dip would be a buying opportunity in MU and QQQ, not a trend change.

Into next week: Light calendar — June 29 has no major data. June 30 brings consumer confidence and Nike earnings. July 1 brings ADP and ISM Manufacturing. The next big question for equities is whether June PCE (reported late July) starts showing the disinflationary impact of oil falling from $114 to $69. If yes, the rally broadens significantly. If no — if services inflation keeps core PCE sticky — the rates headwind intensifies and the market gets more selective. Trade the AI names now. Reassess the broader market in mid-July.
