Market Regime
Chip-Led Risk-Off
A sector-wide semiconductor selloff — triggered by TSMC's Q2 earnings pairing record revenue with a jarring capex reset to $60–$64B and margin guidance below consensus — is dragging the Nasdaq sharply lower (-1.64%) while the broader S&P holds up comparatively (-0.54%). The market is repricing the AI infrastructure trade on the thesis that scaling costs are compressing hardware-sector multiples faster than downstream software monetization can justify them, a concern amplified by ongoing U.S.-Iran tensions keeping oil elevated and Treasury yields sticky near 4.56%. Defensive and consumer-staples names (WMT +2.15%, VZ +2.45%) are catching a rotation bid, while AAPL (+1.76%) is a notable divergence within mega-cap tech — outperforming on its own pre-earnings narrative into its confirmed July 30 report.

Alignment Divergence: One Bullish Hold While Everything Else Fractures

EMA trend alignment splits this watchlist cleanly into two camps today. AAPL and NVDA carry bullish-aligned daily EMAs — price above EMA20, EMA20 above EMA50, EMA50 above EMA200 — yet they are responding to the same session in opposite directions. AAPL at $333.26 is one of the few names in the brief printing green, diverging from the broader tech tape with a +1.76% move and meaningful call-side options activity. NVDA at $207.40 is down -2.40% despite the identical EMA structure, a reminder that bullish EMA alignment describes trend agreement, not insulation from sector-level selling pressure. Trend alignment tells you the tape has been constructive — it does not protect against a high-multiple sector being re-rated on capex shock. SPY also holds bullish alignment and is absorbing the session with relative composure at $750.72 (-0.54%), its EMA structure functioning as a description of the broader market's still-intact longer-term trend rather than a session-direction forecast.

The bearish-aligned and mixed-alignment names are where today's real action is concentrated. QQQ at $705.94 shows mixed EMA alignment, consistent with the Nasdaq sitting in a zone where the daily EMAs have not all resolved in either direction — and -1.64% on the session with 35.8M shares traded reflects the ambiguity in that structure. AMZN ($249.89, -1.99%), INTC ($96.98, -5.84%), HOOD ($106.02, -8.24%), VZ ($43.88, +2.45%), and WMT ($114.95, +2.15%) all carry mixed EMA alignment. What separates them today is catalyst, not alignment: INTC's -5.84% decline on 105.8M shares — meaningfully above its 104.86M average — is the session's clearest earnings-positioning move, with Q2 results confirmed for July 23 AMC. The stock is now 31.8% off its 52-week high of $142.35 and has re-approached the $96–$97 area where Wall Street's consensus target zone had been clustered earlier this year, a coincidence of price and analyst math that is worth noting but that mixed EMA alignment does not independently validate as an entry. HOOD's -8.24% at $106.02 is the largest percentage drop on the board today, and its unusual options structure — calls running roughly 2.6x puts on a sharply down session — is itself a data point, not a direction read.

VZ and WMT sit in the unusual position of mixed EMA alignment but positive price action, both gaining more than 2% as rotation out of high-multiple tech and into yield-and-defense accelerates. At $43.88, VZ is seven days from its July 24 BMO earnings, having beaten EPS estimates in each of its last four quarters with a consensus estimate of $1.27 for Q2. At $114.95, WMT's next event is further out (August 20 BMO), making today's move more purely macro-rotation driven. Mixed EMA alignment on both means the daily trend structure hasn't committed to a direction — it reflects the tug-of-war between a recovering income-name thesis and a market that has been rotating inconsistently between growth and defense all summer.

The structural read for today: SPY's bullish EMA alignment and relative session composure (-0.54%) while QQQ's mixed structure leads the decline (-1.64%) is itself a meaningful divergence. When the index that carries the semiconductor and mega-cap tech weight fractures faster than the broader 500-name index, and when defensive names with mixed alignment are the day's outperformers, the EMA structures are collectively telling a rotation story — one where the highest-multiple, AI-capex-exposed names are doing the damage and the parts of the market less tied to that thesis are holding or gaining. AAPL's bullish alignment and green price action into a confirmed July 30 earnings date is the cleanest on-chart confirmation of that bifurcation.
