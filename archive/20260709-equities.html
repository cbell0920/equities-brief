VIX & Term Structure
17.01
With spot VIX at 17.01 — below its long-term mean of ~19-20 — the futures curve almost certainly remains in standard contango, where longer-dated VIX futures are priced above spot; this is the normal state roughly 80% of the time and reflects the market's structural expectation that near-term vol will revert higher toward the historical mean. At this VIX level, contango creates a persistent roll-yield drag on long-volatility ETFs like VXX and UVXY, while simultaneously offering a statistical edge for premium-selling strategies — though the Fear & Greed reading of 22/100 warrants caution about adding uncovered short-vol exposure.

NVDA's China Reprieve vs. Megacap Earnings Purge: Two Diverging Trades Before the Q2 Storm Hits

The highest-conviction setup today is NVDA long with a defined stop. At $204.12, the stock has recaptured the $200 level on a dual catalyst that is materially new information — Beijing's reported H200 approvals directly address what had been cited as a zero-revenue contribution from China data center compute, and the explicit Kyber denial removes a product-roadmap overhang that had shaved roughly $1 trillion in market cap off NVDA since May highs. Goldman's public framing of 21.7x forward earnings as 'compelling' relative to the company's 85%+ revenue growth rate is the institutional permission slip for re-entry. The 200-day SMA at $191.37 is a clean, well-defined stop. The trade's near-term proof-point is a close above $209 (50-day SMA); if NVDA can clear $212 on a sustained basis, the descending channel that has trapped the stock all summer is broken. Conviction score: 78.

The second setup — and the one that will define the next four weeks — is the short side of META (600.12 close risk) and BAC (57.00 support), both of which face binary earnings events with deteriorating chart structure. META at $603.12 is precisely at the $600 psychological floor, below which the descending channel targets $556 — a 7.8% drawdown before the July 29 print even arrives. The key question the market is asking META and every other AI-capex-heavy hyperscaler is whether $220B+ in collective AI infrastructure spending is translating to revenue uplift, or simply expanding depreciation schedules. BAC at $58.30 on -2.61% with Q2 earnings arriving in five days is a weaker setup technically and fundamentally — NIM compression under a hawkish Fed (Chair Warsh has warned of upside inflation risks; May CPI was 4.2%), credit quality uncertainties, and commercial real estate overhang make any rally into $60 resistance a shorting opportunity.

The macro risk that could blow up both theses simultaneously is a NFLX earnings beat on July 16 that reignites the megacap growth narrative — Netflix reports first among major growth names this season and its results 'tend to set the bar for the rest of the megacap-technology names that report later in the month.' A clean NFLX beat with strong ad-tier guidance could pivot the tape from today's risk-off purge into a relief rally that squeezes META shorts and lifts financials on improved risk appetite. Conversely, a NFLX miss would validate today's de-risking and likely push SPY through the 740.00 support level in a meaningful way.

The wild card that deserves a dedicated line: SPCX at $148.30 is quietly building toward a volatility event that most equity desks are underpricing. The stock is now 18% below the $175.50 threshold needed to trigger the bonus 10% insider unlock, meaning today's price action is actually reducing near-term supply pressure — but the 20% tranche tied to Q2 earnings (late July/early August) remains on the calendar regardless of price level. With only ~5% of 13 billion shares publicly traded, SPCX is structurally a thin-float instrument where the upcoming earnings-linked lockup event will be a binary volatility spike. Position sizing discipline is essential — this is not a name where averaging down on weakness is a viable strategy ahead of the first major supply unlock in three weeks.
