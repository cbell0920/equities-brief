Macro Environment — July 1, H2 Opens

Markets enter H2 2026 with a sharp reversal of the optimism that dominated Q2's final days. Iran ruled out direct talks with the US in Doha, reversing the tentative truce narrative that had driven a powerful risk-on rally Monday and Tuesday. Oil fell 1.1% to $68.71 as a result — Iran's rejection removes the hope that a formal deal might further ease Hormuz tensions. Equity futures fell 0.3–0.5%, USD bid returned, and safe haven flows resumed into CHF and JPY.

The JOLTS report yesterday was the other major market driver — job openings climbed to a two-year high, directly challenging any dovish Fed narrative. With JOLTS pointing to a tight labour market and PCE at 4.1%, the Fed has every reason to maintain its hawkish stance. Markets now price approximately 70% probability of a September rate hike. Fed Chair Warsh speaks at the ECB Forum in Sintra, Portugal at 9:30 AM ET today — the first major Fed communication since the JOLTS data. Any hawkish signal extends USD strength across all pairs. Any dovish surprise would be a significant catalyst for reversal.

Today brings the full data calendar: ADP Employment Change at 8:15 AM ET (forecast 118K, below prior 122K) and ISM Manufacturing PMI at 10:00 AM ET (forecast 53.8). A weak ADP print combined with Iran tensions could create cross-currents — risk-off from geopolitics vs USD softness from weak jobs. A strong ADP confirms the JOLTS signal and extends USD strength. ISM Manufacturing at 53.8 would confirm industrial expansion, hawkish for the Fed.

Q2 closed with historic strength — S&P 500 +14%, Nasdaq +20%, Dow +12%, the best quarter since 2020. Gold fell 14% in Q2 — its worst quarter since Q2 2013. Oil fell 30% in Q2 — its worst quarter since 2020. The commodity complex is under structural pressure from the supply glut building as Iran exports recover post-conflict. Iran says it has shipped 40+ million barrels since the US lifted its naval blockade.

Senior Analyst Note
TPTraders Senior FX Analyst — July 1, 2026

Iran's rejection of Doha talks is the morning's defining development and it confirms the structural USD bull case. The tentative truce optimism that lifted GBP/USD, AUD/USD, and EUR/USD over the past two sessions was always a tactical move, not a structural reversal. Now that the optimism is unwinding, those pairs return toward their structural trends — and those structural trends are all USD-bullish. Short EUR/USD, short GBP/USD, and long USD/CAD are the cleanest expressions. The setups are back on offer at better levels than last week.

Warsh at Sintra at 9:30 AM ET is the most important single event of the day. The JOLTS data yesterday showed job openings at a two-year high — this is unambiguously hawkish context for any Fed speaker. If Warsh confirms the September hike is on the table, September hike probability moves from 70% toward 85%+, and EUR/USD breaks below 1.1300 today. Watch his language on inflation expectations and the balance sheet — Warsh has previously campaigned for bond-selling (QT) and any announcement of new task forces to evaluate the balance sheet would be an additional USD-positive signal.

ADP data at 8:15 AM lands before Warsh speaks. The forecast is 118K — below the prior 122K. If ADP surprises to the downside significantly (below 80K), there's a brief window where USD softens before Warsh speaks. That window — if it opens — is a buying opportunity in USD/CAD and a shorting opportunity in EUR/USD, not a trend change. The data-driven macro is too strongly USD-bullish to be reversed by a single ADP print.

The USD/CAD setup into NFP tomorrow is the highest-conviction trade of the week. Every data point is pointing the same direction: CAD is structurally weak (oil at $68.71 down 30% in Q2, spec longs dangerously exposed, fundamentals bearish), USD is structurally strong (JOLTS 2yr high, PCE 4.1%, Warsh hawkish, ADP and ISM today). A strong NFP tomorrow likely breaks USD/CAD above 1.4300, opening the path to 1.4450. Build or hold longs through today's data and into tomorrow morning's print.
