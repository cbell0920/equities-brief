Two high-conviction setups today — and one landmine that could reset the week

The highest-conviction long setup today is META at $669.21. This is not a chase — it is a structural re-rating with a specific fundamental catalyst (AI cloud monetization + Iris chip September production target) and a hard upcoming catalyst in the July 29 earnings print. The stock still trades at a trailing P/E of approximately 21-23x, a significant discount to the Nasdaq Composite's ~39x trailing multiple, despite Q1 revenue of $56.3B growing 33% YoY and operating income of $22.9B. The $640 level represents the prior-week breakout; any intraday pullback toward that level today on profit-taking is a potential entry. The risk: cloud margins will likely be thinner than the ad business, and Wall Street is already baking in $135-155B in 2026 capex. If Zuckerberg raises the capex ceiling again at Q2 earnings without delivering cloud revenue clarity, the stock will give back gains violently. Size accordingly.

The second setup — more asymmetric and higher risk — is INTC at $109.84. The stock has fallen approximately 21% from its $140 peak following the Bank of America semiconductor bubble report and 18A yield delay concerns. It is now testing the $108.60 support level which corresponds to the 200-day EMA vicinity. The July 23 Q2 earnings report is 10 trading days away, and the setup is a classic binary: Intel guided for $13.8-14.8B in Q2 revenue and non-GAAP EPS of $0.20, against a backdrop where Q1 delivered a 23.6% post-earnings rally on a meaningful beat. The put/call OI ratio at 1.02 — rare near-parity — means the options market is pricing both outcomes with roughly equal probability. HSBC's $200 price target (citing foundry long-term optionality) versus the $25 bear case reflects analyst consensus dispersion that is virtually unmatched in our current coverage universe. A long here is viable only with a defined stop at $107 and a clear willingness to hold through the binary event; a straddle is the cleaner expression for those who want to own the vol without a directional bet.

The key risk to watch for the broader session is NFLX on Thursday July 16. At $73.37 and -2.78% today on 46.7M shares, the tape is already distributing. A Q2 EPS miss or weak Q3 guide from Netflix would likely bleed into the GOOG and broader Communication Services complex — given that sector ETFs like XLC carry both names at significant combined weight. This cross-contamination risk is real: a bad Netflix print the same week as INTC earnings (July 23) and META earnings (July 29) could create a rolling tech earnings anxiety wave through the rest of July. The VIX at 16.43 is elevated enough to warrant attention but not yet dislocating — that changes quickly if NFLX Thursday disappoints on subscriber metrics.

Finally, SPCX at $145.30 deserves active monitoring rather than a directional call. The stock is currently $30.20 below the $175.50 performance trigger that would unlock an additional 10% of locked shares alongside Q2 earnings. If SPCX cannot reclaim $160-175 in the next few weeks, the bonus tranche does not trigger — which is actually a relative positive for existing float holders, as it means less supply enters the market at the first earnings unlock. Traders should watch the 10-day rolling price going into the Q2 earnings date; it is one of the more precise mechanical supply-vs-demand signals available in the current IPO cycle.
