Macro Context — H2 Opens July 1

The second half of 2026 opens with the market absorbing three simultaneous forces: Iran's diplomatic reversal, a data-heavy day, and profit-taking after a historic Q2. The S&P 500 closed Q2 up 14%, Nasdaq up 20%, and Dow up 12% — the best quarter since 2020. After a performance like that, the natural H2 opener includes institutional rebalancing and profit-taking, which helps explain why futures are -0.3% to -0.5% even without the Iran news adding additional pressure.

Iran's rejection of Doha talks is the session's primary risk catalyst. Yesterday's optimism about a formal peace deal is evaporating. Tehran ruled out direct talks with US delegates in Qatar. This removes the near-term path to a formal resolution of the Hormuz situation and reintroduces geopolitical risk premium into markets. Oil has already responded (-1.1% to $68.71). The broader equity market reaction is more muted but the direction of risk appetite is clearly negative.

The data calendar today is significant. ADP Employment Change at 8:15 AM ET (forecast 118K) and ISM Manufacturing PMI at 10:00 AM ET (forecast 53.8) both land before Fed Chair Warsh speaks at 9:30 AM ET at the ECB Forum in Sintra. This creates a busy 90-minute window from 8:15 to 10:00 AM ET where three major catalysts overlap. Prepare for elevated volatility and potentially contradictory moves — data driving one direction while Warsh commentary drives another.

NFP tomorrow (Thursday July 2) remains the week's defining event. Forecast: 180K-200K. JOLTS yesterday at a two-year high suggests the labour market is tighter than expected, raising the probability that NFP beats the forecast. A strong NFP above 200K would push September hike probability from 70% toward 90% and likely pressure growth stocks further while benefiting financials. Markets close Friday for Independence Day.

Senior Analyst Note
TPTraders Senior Equities Analyst — July 1, 2026

H2 opens exactly the way smart traders should expect — with a speed bump. Markets don't go up in straight lines, and after a quarter where the S&P 500 gained 14% and the Nasdaq gained 20%, a pause entering the second half is healthy, not alarming. The Iran rejection of Doha talks, the natural H2 rebalancing flows, and a data-heavy day all combine to create a cautious open. This is not a reversal of the bull market. It's a normal reset after a historic quarter.

The most important event of today's session is not the data — it's Warsh at Sintra at 9:30 AM ET. The JOLTS report yesterday showed job openings at a two-year high. Warsh is speaking at the European Central Bank Forum in Portugal immediately after ADP lands. If he acknowledges the tight labour market and signals September is on the table — which the data supports — the Fed hawkish narrative gets a significant boost. Financials (BAC, V) benefit directly. Gold extends its bear. USD extends its rally. Growth stocks face additional multiple compression pressure.

SPCX and GOOG are today's structural standouts on the long side. Wedbush's $190 target initiation on SpaceX from Dan Ives is not a small call — it's institutional validation of the hyperscaler thesis from one of Wall Street's most influential tech analysts. Combined with the July 7 Nasdaq-100 inclusion creating forced buying, SPCX has two independent structural tailwinds. GOOG's Dow inclusion creates similar structural demand. Both are cleaner longs than chasing the broad market on a data-heavy, risk-off day.

NFLX at $71.38 near its 52-week low ($70.86) deserves a closer look with earnings on July 16. The bar is low — the stock is down dramatically from its highs, analyst consensus is overwhelmingly bullish (37 Buy, 0 Sell), and Q1 actually beat estimates significantly. If the stock holds $70.86 support through today's risk-off and into next week, a pre-earnings long with a tight stop is a reasonable setup. The July 16 earnings event is a known catalyst with a clear invalidation level.

Avoid MU today. Burry's short disclosure doesn't change the fundamental AI memory demand thesis, but it changes sentiment in the short term.
